The Moroccan Pastilla

Description

Pastilla, or Bastilla (بسطيلة), is a traditional Moroccan pie featuring a sweet and savory balance of flavors and textures, typically made with slow-cooked chicken, caramelized onions, and almonds, all wrapped in layers of thin, crispy phyllo (also known as warqa or warga) pastry.

It is often spiced with cinnamon and ginger and finished with a dusting of powdered sugar and cinnamon, served at special occasions as a symbol of hospitality and tradition.

Ingredients:

  • Boneless, skinless chicken thighs.
  • Extra virgin olive oil.
  • Sliced yellow onion and minced garlic.
  • Pitted medjool dates, chopped.
  • Toasted sliced almonds.
  • Fresh parsley.
  • Eggs.
  • Phyllo dough.
  • All-purpose Moroccan spices for filling.

Steps:

  1. Brown the chicken.
  2. Cook the chicken.
  3. Shred the chicken.
  4. Add eggs.
  5. Arrange the phyllo dough in the skillet.
  6. Add the chicken in the skillet.
  7. Bake the pie.
  8. Enjoy!
